Hertz Mobile App

2018-2019

The old Hertz app was outdated, crashed frequently, and required development in order to update content. In partnering with IBM, our team worked with the Marketing, UX, and Creative Services teams to deliver the new Hertz Mobile App. The new app gives customers a faster rental experience, allowing users to login with touch ID, manage their rewards, and find nearby locations in an updated user interface. My role was to help provide the content and design templates that could be used to collaborate with the brand and development teams. I also played a role in marketing the launch of the app by building landing pages and posting banners and videos on the website to help drive awareness.

Team Members & Collaborators

IBM Garage, UX, Loyalty, Analytics team, Marketing & Global Stakeholders

Tools

Photoshop, Adobe XD, Adobe Experience Manager, Excel

Old Hertz App

In order to meet customer expectations and reduce calls to the call center, the app was designed to address top questions asked by customers. The plan was to launch with these core features but to continue to iterate to deliver new functionality to meet market and consumer demands.



While the design and layout was built by the IBM development team along with our in-house UX and design teams, one of my roles was to create imagery templates that could be used to ensure there was a consistent visual appearance and brand reinforcement throughout the app.


Hertz rebranded in 2017 and the new look was modernized with bright airy photography and pops of yellow. While this color scheme was eye-catching and a great opportunity for brand reinforcement, it made it tough to find imagery that had high enough contrast to pass web accessibility standards when overlaid with text. We had to be very careful we were adhering to color-blind standards.

45%

Decrease in load time

The new reservation flow rolled out to US and Canadian customers in early March, when covid-19 shutdowns began. The virus took a direct hit on the travel industry making it hard to measure the impact the redesign had on conversions and eCommerce revenue. However, we were able to measure page load speed right away, dropping from 6 seconds to 3-4 seconds- a 45% decrease.


I really enjoyed being a part of each stage in the website migration and redesign- to be involved on the content side of developing and launching new features, gathering feedback, then making changes based on our findings. More often, the small changes had the biggest impact. Removing unnecessary steps and content from the checkout process was a primary driver in increased conversion rates.


Creating consistent page templates and a single design system is vital for a unified brand experience, as we learned, but it's also important to build templates and components that allow for as much flexibility as possible as priorities and marketing initiatives change over time.
